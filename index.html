    1. Simply Supported with Point Load (offset 'a' from left, 'b' from right):
    - Reactions: $R_A = \frac{P \cdot b}{L},\ R_B = \frac{P \cdot a}{L}$
    - Max Bending Moment: $M_{max} = \frac{P \cdot a \cdot b}{L}$ (at loading point)
    - Max deflection: calculated using Macualay's Method or Singularity functions.
    2. Simply Supported with UDL (w):
    - Reactions: $R_A = R_B = \frac{w \cdot L}{2}$
    - Max Bending Moment: $M_{max} = \frac{w \cdot L^2}{8}$ (at center)
    - Max Deflection: $\delta_{max} = \frac{5 \cdot w \cdot L^4}{384 \cdot E \cdot I}$
    3. Cantilever with End Point Load (P):
    - Max Bending Moment: $M_{max} = P \cdot L$ (at fixed end)
    - Max Deflection: $\delta_{max} = \frac{P \cdot L^3}{3 \cdot E \cdot I}$ (at free end).

    Civil Engineering Quick Reference Manual

    1. Concrete Mix Design (IS 456 Guidelines):

    Proportion ratios of cement, fine aggregate (sand), and coarse aggregate by dry volume:

    • M5 (1:5:10): Lean concrete, foundations base PCC.
    • M10 (1:3:6): Plain Concrete (PCC), leveling course.
    • M15 (1:2:4): Floor beds, shallow foundations.
    • M20 (1:1.5:3): RCC Slabs, beams, standard columns.
    • M25 (1:1:2): High-strength RCC columns, footings, water retention tanks.

    2. Rebar Unit Weights & Standards:

    Theoretical weight of steel rebar is calculated by formula: Weight (kg/m) = Dia² / 162.2

    • 8 mm Rebar: ~ 0.395 kg/m (Stirrups & Slab distribution bars)
    • 10 mm Rebar: ~ 0.617 kg/m (Slab main bars)
    • 12 mm Rebar: ~ 0.888 kg/m (Standard residential slab/beams)
    • 16 mm Rebar: ~ 1.578 kg/m (Structural columns & beams)
    • 20 mm Rebar: ~ 2.466 kg/m (Heavy column reinforcements)

    3. Brick Masonry Standards:

    Standard masonry principles assume:

    • Modular Indian brick size: 190 x 90 x 90 mm. With mortar: 200 x 100 x 100 mm.
    • Traditional brick size: 229 x 112 x 70 mm (9" x 4.5" x 3").
    • 1 m³ of brickwork needs approximately 500 modular bricks.
    • Mortar volume occupies approximately 25% to 30% of the gross masonry volume.

    4. Earthwork Slope Allowances (Safety Guidelines):

    To prevent soil collapse during excavation, the sides are sloped back:

    • Stable Rock: 0.25:1 to 0.5:1 (Horizontal : Vertical)
    • Stiff Clay/Hard Soil: 0.5:1 to 0.75:1
    • Average cohesive loam soil: 1:1 (45° angle)
    • Dry Sandy/Loose soil: 1.5:1 to 2:1
